# Calculator Premises

The public IO Calculator is built on two explicit working premises.

Premise 1:
we live inside a black hole, and the CMB is the event horizon, with Hawking radiation falling inward and being observed from the interior.

Premise 2:
the physics inside our black hole are the same as the physics outside our black hole.

These premises are model-building assumptions for the Interior Observer Framework. They are not claimed here as independently established observational theorems.

The calculator uses the claim-discipline labels defined throughout the theorem dictionary:

- `derived`
- `verified`
- `conditional`
- `reconstruction`
- `speculative`

Every public output card and theorem node should state which of those labels applies.
