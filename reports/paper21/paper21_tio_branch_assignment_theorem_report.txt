Paper 21 - T_IO Branch Assignment Theorem

Status boundary
---------------
- derived / scoped theorem: GTTP is confined to the RT/BY optical readout class.
- derived / scoped theorem: once BBN is typed as local bulk thermodynamics, it evaluates at T_IO, not T_obs.

Executive answer
----------------
Yes, the branch assignment closes in scoped theorem form.
The key point is that GTTP was never a theorem about replacing the bulk thermal scale everywhere.
It was derived in Paper 17 as a modular/frequency readout theorem for boundary photon observables,
and Paper 19 / Paper 21 already localize that readout to the RT/BY optical class.

Theorem 21.L (T_IO branch assignment theorem on the reduced bulk-thermodynamic class)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Status: derived / scoped theorem

Premises
1. L1. Paper 17 Corollary 17.7: GTTP is the modular readout theorem T_obs = T_IO x^K_gauge, derived from uniform frequency rescaling of the boundary-photon readout variable omega.
2. L2. Paper 19 RI4/RI5/FS8: the observable architecture distinguishes local bulk observables from boundary-calibrated optical observables; not every realizable observable is optical, but the scorecard optical class is.
3. L3. Paper 21.J: on the reduced RT/BY optical class, SU(2) averaging is a genuine measurement filter and the optical observable is evaluated after projection to the reduced fixed-point sector.
4. L4. Paper 19 JC2: the BBN baryon-loading slot is the alpha = 1 inventory branch.
5. L5. Minimal class-membership premise TIO1: BBN abundance/rate observables are local bulk thermodynamic observables of the plasma, with no primitive RT/BY optical readout leg.

Minimal reduced object
    M_BBN^red = M_BBN^red := A_rxn bar-tensor M_th
    A_rxn is the gauge-neutral local reaction/rate algebra for the plasma species, and M_th is the Paper 17 thermal photon algebra at the base IO KMS scale. This is the minimal reduced bulk-thermodynamic object needed to state BBN without an observer readout leg.

Statement
    Under L1-L5, local bulk thermodynamic observables are evaluated at the base IO thermal scale T_IO, whereas the GTTP factor x^K_gauge belongs only to the RT/BY optical readout class. Therefore the BBN branch uses T_IO, not T_obs. Equivalently: inserting T_obs into the BBN eta conversion misclassifies a bulk thermodynamic observable as an observer-side optical readout.

Proof skeleton
1. By L1, GTTP is not an arbitrary temperature renormalization. It is the modular/frequency readout theorem for the boundary-photon readout variable omega.
2. By L2, the stack already distinguishes local bulk observables from boundary-calibrated optical observables; the optical class is not universal.
3. By L3, the RT/BY optical class is an observer readout class: its expectations are evaluated after the optical projection/filter E_opt and therefore belong to the boundary measurement apparatus.
4. By L5, BBN abundance/rate observables belong instead to the local bulk thermodynamic class M_BBN^red and have no primitive RT/BY optical slot.
5. Therefore the GTTP readout map has no primitive leg on which to act in BBN. Applying x^K_gauge there would move the observable into a different class than the one stipulated by L5 and L2.
6. The thermal scale entering the BBN plasma state is therefore the base KMS scale T_IO. By L4, the baryon-loading slot on that branch is alpha = 1.

Assessment of the only new premise
    L5 is much weaker than the old branch-assignment claim. It does not assert T_IO vs T_obs. It only classifies BBN as what it operationally is: a local bulk thermodynamic process rather than an observer-side optical readout.

Boundary 21.L2
--------------
Status: derived / scope boundary
Statement: The theorem is class-specific, not universal. It does not say every thermodynamic-looking quantity in the framework uses T_IO. It says only: if the primitive observable is local bulk thermodynamics with no RT/BY optical leg, then GTTP does not apply to it.
Not proved:
- The theorem does not derive Delta N_eff itself.
- The theorem does not prove anything about a future unreduced BBN operator algebra that explicitly contains an observer readout leg.
- The theorem does not move late optical thermometry such as FIRAS off the T_obs branch.

Bottom line
-----------
The old mixed statement
    "BBN uses T_IO because that fit worked computationally"
can now be replaced by the stronger theorem-grade statement
    "GTTP is an observer-side optical readout theorem; BBN is a local bulk thermodynamic class; therefore BBN uses T_IO."

So the surviving conditionality in the 3.4062 scorecard is the radiation-value theorem,
not the temperature-branch assignment.
