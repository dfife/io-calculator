Run 19: 21-cm Absorption at z ~ 17
EDGES reference: Bowman et al. 2018, Nature 555, 67-70 (https://doi.org/10.1038/nature25792).
Derived no-go:
  adiabatic gas cooling on the local OS clock obeys d ln T_g / d tau = -2 d ln a / d tau,
  so T_g is proportional to a^(-2). Extra OS proper time does not by itself make T_g lower at fixed z.
Age ratio at z = 17: tau_OS / t_LCDM = 1.476292 (0.333101 / 0.225634 Gyr).
Reduced-model LCDM floor estimate: z_dec = 142.050, T_g = 6.173 K, T21 = -251.368 mK.
Schur + omega_b,geom: z_dec = 145.176, T_g = 6.041 K, T21 = -230.009 mK.
Schur + omega_b,eff:  T21 = -317.517 mK.
Schur + omega_b,clustering: T21 = -186.036 mK.
If one anchors the flat baseline to a canonical -200 mK instead of the adiabatic-floor reduced model, Schur + omega_b,geom maps to -183.0 mK and Schur + omega_b,eff maps to -252.6 mK.
Interpretation: the OS proper-time surplus is real, but it is not the control variable for adiabatic cooling at fixed redshift. Geometry alone does not reproduce an EDGES-depth trough.
