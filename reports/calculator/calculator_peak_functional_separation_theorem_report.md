Theorem 37.PK1 (background-ratio / peak-functional separation theorem)
---------------------------------------------------------------------

Premises

1. Paper 20 / 21 fix the physical acoustic-angle measurement chain as

       primitive sky field -> harmonic coefficients -> C_ell -> A_peak.

2. Paper 21.B6 proves `theta*` can inherit gauge content only from the primitive one-slot stage; `A_peak` itself is gauge-neutral.

3. Paper 32.B fixes the full linearized IO transfer as a typed lower-triangular map whose perturbation/readout side is not reduced to the source block alone.

4. Paper 31 source-operator and local acoustic-family scans show that large perturbation/source modifications can materially change TTTEEE `chi2` while leaving the derived background ratio `100theta_s` unchanged on a fixed branch.

Statement

The thermodynamic/background ratio

    100theta_s = 100 r_s(z_rec) / D_M(z_rec)

is not, by itself, the exact physical peak-position readout `theta*_peak`.
Therefore theorem-grade numeric closure of `theta*_peak` cannot be obtained from the background ratio alone.
It requires either the exact `A_peak` / closed-`S^3` perturbation readout, or a separate theorem identifying `A_peak` with that background ratio on the relevant scope.

Proof

The Paper 20 / 21 measurement chain places `theta*_peak` downstream of the primitive sky field and the peak functional `A_peak`, not at the raw thermodynamic ratio alone.

Paper 21.B6 shows the gauge content descends from the primitive one-slot stage, but that does not collapse the numerical readout to the background ratio by itself.

Paper 32.B then fixes the complete IO transfer as a typed map with an unresolved perturbation/readout side beyond the source block.

Finally, the Paper 31 scans exhibit fixed-branch cases with identical `100theta_s` but wildly different TTTEEE likelihoods under source and local ODE changes. So the background ratio does not uniquely determine the actual peak readout.

Therefore no theorem may identify the physical numeric `theta*` with the background ratio unless an additional peak/readout identification theorem is supplied. QED.
